Reformed Faith in the Golden Triangle of Eustis, Mount Dora and Tavares, Florida


This page is to give local readers a small sampling of concepts associated with the Reformed faith, it is my hope that it may lead you to read more about the Reformed faith and perhaps come to a local Bible study or attend a local Reformed church.

Reformed Christian beliefs by some known as historic Christian beliefs go back to the time of the reformation, also called the Protestant Reformation, around the years 1500 to mid 1600. Most attribute Martin Luther's Ninety=Five Theses to be the catalyst for the Reformation but Reformed sentiments abounded then. The time was ripe not only in Germany but also in Switzerland with Ulrich Zwingli and many others.

Here are a few names worth mentioning of those who have had reformed beliefs, but this is not a comprehensive list nor does it span all of reformed history.

  • Martin Luther of Germany
  • Ulrich Zwingli of Switzerland
  • John Calvin of France
  • Geerhardus Johannes Vos of Netherlands to USA
  • John Gresham Machen of USA
  • John Piper of USA
  • James White of USA
  • R.C. Sproul of USA

From the reformation onward come denominations we still know today such as Anglicans, Presbyterians, Lutherans, and Baptists and many others. Some of these denominations or splitter groups from them still retain what may truly be called Reformed beliefs such as the OPC - Orthodox Presbyterian Church, Reformed Baptists and a number of affiliated and non-affiliated churches.

In their quest for retention of doctrinal purity many of these reformed churches have had to isolate themselves from larger denominations that tend to lean toward more liberal beliefs over time. Such a schism is happening in the Methodist Church at this time, 2018-2019. I personally know Methodists on both sides of the line, the controversial subject being acceptance or rejection of LGBTQ clergy. Again, it is likely that the more conservative group will be the smaller and will simply leave the larger group, maybe even leaving the name behind, who knows. Historic Christianity has been chased out of many churches especially since the onslaught of liberal thought in the 1800s introduced by people like Friedrich Nietzsche, Soren Kierkegaard or Karl Marx. Their views and those of others like them influenced society to the extent that people tried to apply them to the Bible where they do not belong. New religious movments also influenced Christianity and even Bible commentaries. The Scofield Bible prevalent about 1920 spread non-historic interpretation of the Bible that caused many churches to leave historic Reformed doctrine in favor of a watered-down version of biblical interpretation. The Christian Church at the time went through a paradigm shift. It will take just such a shift in thought to return to historic apostolic doctrine. The Pauline letters most prevalently, are thoroughly Reformed.

To clarify at least a little what Reformed beliefs are, I have decided to use an acronym called Tulip to explain. Granted, some people don't believe every tenet but may lean toward reformed beliefs, but then they are not Reformed.

What is Tulip?
Term
Explanation
T
Total Depravity
The human race, every individual is born sinful, everyone needs atonement for their sins. No one is able to turn to God on their own for salvation, no one can even want to turn to God for salvation without God's intervention.
U
Unconditional Election
God chooses those He will save not based on any behavior or attitude of the person but solely by His will. There is no injustice in this since no one deserves salvation and God's universe is His creation so that it is His right to determine what happens to it and to us.
L
Limited Atonement
The blood of Jesus Christ atones for the sins of the elect, those that God has chosen.
I
Irresistible Grace
God's grace overcomes the natural resistance of those that he has chosen and they will be His children because He wills it.
P
Perseverence of the Saints
Those that have been chosen by God to be His children and saved by grace will persevere in the faith to the end of their lives and not lose salvation. From a human perspective this would be evidenced by the proper behavior and attitudes. Salvation cannot be lost by those who have it. This is for most of the reformed the most comforting concept, that God having saved a soul will not let it go.

The main idea in Reformed theology is that from the study of the Bible one is to gain perspective into God's way of looking at things, that is a solid, biblical, Reformed way of thought that most certainly will conflict with a purely human way of looking at things, which is more and more the common flow of thought today as the world influences the churches, what could be called liberal thought that is often concerned with concepts of fairness and equity. These concepts may work on some levels from a human to human perspective but it is not the way God sees things and not the way He operates according to the Bible. He is not asking for our approval and He does not need to.
